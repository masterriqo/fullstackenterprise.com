DGTL-ECON:201

Break the Old Script. Learn to Play the New Game.

Subscribe for Digital Economics Updates

Course Objectives

  • Understand the historical and economic shifts from 1960s to 2025.
  • Learn to leverage digital tools and information for wealth creation.
  • Master practical strategies for self-education, freelancing, and digital business.
  • Redefine time, liberty, and risk in the modern economy.

Course Modules

Module 1: History & Context

  • Why the “get a second job” advice used to work.
  • Wages, pensions, and dollar strength then vs now.
  • Show the math: 1960s vs 2025 household economics.
  • Conclusion: the rules changed.

Module 2: Economics for Survival

  • Liabilities vs assets.
  • The cap of time-for-money work.
  • Leverage used by corporations & wealthy individuals.
  • The consumerism trap.

Module 3: The Digital Shift

  • Information is free, skills are democratized.
  • Remote work, creator economy, automation.
  • Your laptop = more powerful than a factory.

Module 4: The New Pathways

  • Education 2.0: certifications & micro-learning.
  • Building a portfolio (GitHub, blog, YouTube, etc.).
  • Direct selling: freelance, consulting, courses.
  • Incorporating yourself as a business.

Module 5: Practical Implementation

  • Cutting liabilities & wasted time.
  • Investing in internet, skills, and scalable tools.
  • First leverage moves: services, products, automation.
  • Scaling: $300 → $3,000 online strategies.

Module 6: Liberation

  • Redefining leisure & time freedom.
  • True liberty in the 21st century.
  • Reframing risk: the danger of staying trapped.
